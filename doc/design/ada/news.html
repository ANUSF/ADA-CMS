Steve McEachern is the new Deputy Director of ADA

9 September 2009

Steve McEachern is the new Deputy Director of ADA. Steve has previously worked as a lecturer in the areas of management and organisational behaviour at the Universities of Ballarat and Curtin. He also established the University of Ballarat’s Survey Research Facility, funded through an ARC-LIEF grant in collaboration with Deakin University in 2003, which provided telephone and mail survey data collection systems for projects conducted by staff and students.Read more

Longitudinal Surveys of Australian Youth

30 July 2009

Accessing and using data from the Longitudinal Surveys of Australian Youth: current and potential users of the LSAY data are encouraged to attend a one-day data workshop hosted by the National Centre for Vocational Education Research. Participants will gain a better understanding of the usefulness of the LSAY data as well as its limitations. The newly developed cohort reports will be showcased. There will also be opportunity for participation through exercises focusing on how the LSAY data can be used to answer real-life research questions. The data workshop will be held in Melbourne on Tuesday 1 September, 9.30 - 4.00pm. A fee of $180 (inc. GST) applies. For further information and registration see LSAY events.Read more

Funding is available for organisations

29 June 2009

Funding is available for organisations who are not members of ACSPRI to access data from the Longitudinal Surveys of Australian Youth (LSAY), a research program that tracks young people from around age 15 to 25 as they move from school to post-school destinations. See How to access LSAY data for more details. The funds are provided by the federal Department of Education, Employment and Workplace Relations through the LSAY Research Innovation and Expansion Fund managed by the National Centre for Vocational Education Research.Read more

ADA Nesstar Catalogue has now been consolidated

13 May 2009

The ADA Nesstar Catalogue has now been consolidated so that all of the data are held on one server. The topic classification folders now form the top-level view replacing the ANU, UQ, UWA and UNSW folder views. The non-Australian studies are now rearranged into views by country. These changes should improve resource discovery and require less drilling down through folders to locate data. Read more
